\begin{abstract}
We implement an end-to-end computational framework for sudden $\theta$-quenches in the $(1+1)$D lattice Schwinger model with staggered fermions and open boundaries, mapped to qubits after Gauss-law elimination. For small chains ($N\lesssim 12$), we compute exact ground states, unitary post-quench dynamics, Loschmidt diagnostics, connected correlators, bipartite entanglement, and stabilizer-Renyi magic $M_\alpha$. We then fit time snapshots with multiple neural quantum state families (autoregressive GRU, MADE, exact-normalized RBM, and an independent baseline) and quantify learnability by validation NLL, KL, TV, and convergence speed. Stage-1 yields a \emph{scoped} claim: strong small-$N$ baseline evidence for a magic--learnability link with explicit regime/size boundaries. Stage-2 adds cross-model tests (Schwinger + TFIM), finite-size trend diagnostics, and bound-style checks; an all-architecture entropy-controlled beta-law is supported in the tested corridor, while a universal correlation-sign law fails and remains architecture-dependent.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
We study whether non-stabilizerness (quantified by stabilizer-Renyi magic) predicts neural learnability degradation in real-time lattice gauge dynamics. The objective is a \emph{decision-safe} empirical claim: identify when the effect is present, and map where it fails.

\section{Related work}
Our setup combines three strands: (i) Schwinger-model quench diagnostics (Loschmidt and correlator structure), (ii) stabilizer-magic complexity measures in many-body states, and (iii) neural quantum state fitting of measurement distributions. The contribution here is the integrated claim-gated pipeline linking all three with explicit boundary analysis.

\section{Model and Hamiltonian decomposition}
We work with staggered matter on $N$ sites and open boundaries. The spin Hamiltonian is decomposed as
\begin{equation}
H(\theta)=H_{\pm}+H_{ZZ}+H_Z+H_\mathrm{const},
\end{equation}
with
\begin{align}
H_{\pm} &= \frac{1}{2a}\sum_{n=0}^{N-2}\left(\sigma_n^+\sigma_{n+1}^-+\sigma_n^-\sigma_{n+1}^+\right)
=\frac{1}{4a}\sum_{n=0}^{N-2}(X_nX_{n+1}+Y_nY_{n+1}),\\
H_Z &= \frac{m}{2}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}(-1)^n Z_n + \text{(linear terms from electric energy)}.
\end{align}
Using Gauss' law with background field $\alpha=\theta/(2\pi)$,
\begin{equation}
L_n = \alpha + \sum_{k=0}^n q_k,\qquad q_k = \frac{Z_k+(-1)^k}{2},
\end{equation}
and electric energy coefficient $\frac{g^2a}{2}$, we obtain nonlocal $ZZ$ couplings and additional $Z$ shifts. The implementation computes explicit pair and linear coefficients induced by the open-boundary kernel $w_{ij}=N-1-\max(i,j)$.

\section{Quench protocol and exact dynamics}
Given $(m,g,a)$, initial angle $\theta_0$, and final angle $\theta_1$, we:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Build $H(\theta_0)$ and compute its ground state $\ket{\psi_0}$ via exact diagonalization.
    \item Evolve under $H(\theta_1)$:
    \begin{equation}
    \ket{\psi(t)}=e^{-iH(\theta_1)t}\ket{\psi_0},
    \end{equation}
    using dense eigensystem propagation for tiny Hilbert spaces and Krylov \texttt{expm\_multiply} otherwise.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Measured observables and diagnostics}
For each time point we compute:
\begin{align}
\epsilon(t)&=\frac{1}{N}\bra{\psi(t)}H(\theta_1)\ket{\psi(t)},\\
M(t)&=\frac{1}{N}\sum_n(-1)^n\expval{Z_n}_t,\\
C_{ij}(t)&=\expval{Z_iZ_j}_t-\expval{Z_i}_t\expval{Z_j}_t,\\
\lambda(t)&=-\frac{1}{N}\log\left|\braket{\psi_0|\psi(t)}\right|^2,
\end{align}
plus bipartite von Neumann entropy $S_A(t)$ at the half-chain cut.

\section{Stabilizer-Renyi magic}
For each Pauli string $P\in\{I,X,Y,Z\}^{\otimes n}$ and pure state $\ket{\psi}$,
\begin{equation}
c_P=\expval{P},\qquad \Xi_P=\frac{1}{d}c_P^2,\qquad d=2^n.
\end{equation}
We evaluate
\begin{equation}
M_\alpha(\psi)=\frac{1}{1-\alpha}\log\sum_P\Xi_P^\alpha-\log d,
\end{equation}
with emphasis on $\alpha=2$. The code streams through Pauli strings in symplectic $(x,z)$ representation and batches parity evaluations.

\section{NNQS learnability experiment}
Snapshots $\{\ket{\psi(t_k)}\}$ are sampled in the computational basis from $p(z)=|\psi_z|^2$. We train three model classes to learn $p_\theta(z)$ by minimizing NLL: an autoregressive GRU, an exactly normalized RBM (small-$N$), and an independent Bernoulli baseline. Metrics:
\begin{itemize}
    \item validation NLL,
    \item exact KL$\left(p\Vert p_\theta\right)$ on all $2^N$ bitstrings,
    \item total variation distance,
    \item epochs to threshold NLL.
\end{itemize}
We regress validation NLL/KL/TV versus snapshot $M_2$ and apply bootstrap/permutation/meta-analysis claim gates.
In the generated figure set, the primary scatter uses final validation NLL versus $M_2$, while exact KL and TV are retained in accompanying CSV outputs.

\section{Validation checks}
The pipeline enforces and stores:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Hermiticity of $H(\theta_0)$ and $H(\theta_1)$,
    \item norm conservation drift $\max_t\left|\lVert\psi(t)\rVert^2-1\right|$,
    \item dense-vs-Krylov trajectory mismatch for small-$N$,
    \item magic sanity states: $\ket{0}^{\otimes n}$ and GHZ (low magic), $\ket{T}^{\otimes n}$ (higher magic).
\end{itemize}

\section{Robustness and claim scope}
Baseline ($N=6,8$) cross-architecture runs support \texttt{magic $\rightarrow$ validation NLL} and the entropy-controlled variant. A higher-seed full-$\theta$ rerun remains supported. However, stronger $N=10$ exact-magic stress runs and multi-regime replications show that this relation is not universal under current settings. The defensible statement is therefore conditional: magic--learnability coupling is regime- and size-dependent in this small-$N$ exact study.

Stage-1 gate status is tracked in a standardized effect registry (\texttt{outputs/stage1\_prxq/stage1/effect\_registry.json}) with explicit flags for headline-region support and documented boundary failures.

\section{Stage-2 cross-model generalization analysis}
To stress-test cross-model generalization, we augment Stage-1 Schwinger outputs with a second many-body family (TFIM), and evaluate a dynamical corridor defined by large Loschmidt-rate conditions. We report:
\begin{itemize}
    \item pooled Fisher-$z$ meta-effects for the primary endpoint \texttt{magic $\rightarrow$ val NLL $|$ entropy},
    \item bootstrap confidence bounds for the entropy-controlled slope $\beta_\mathrm{magic}$ in
    \(
    \mathrm{valNLL}\sim 1+\mathrm{magic}+S_A,
    \)
    \item finite-size trend fits versus $1/N$,
    \item Pinsker sanity (\( \mathrm{KL}\ge 2\,\mathrm{TV}^2\)).
\end{itemize}

The all-architecture beta-law gate passes (\(\beta_\mathrm{magic}>0\) with confidence and quantile robustness), including a cluster-robust mechanism regression with positive lower bounds for each architecture. However, the all-architecture correlation-sign gate fails due heterogeneity in one weak TFIM baseline, while the expressive-only sign gate (GRU/MADE) passes across tested corridor quantiles.

\section{Stage-3 adversarial extension (third model family)}
We then perform a stricter adversarial extension by adding XXZ quenches and testing two null-disproof gates at cell level (model family $\times$ architecture): (i) bootstrap CI lower bound on $\beta_\mathrm{magic}$ and (ii) one-sided permutation null with BH-FDR correction. We include an XXZ regime tuning plus size stress through $N=12$ using an approximate-magic calibration envelope, and evaluate the expressive architecture subset (GRU/MADE).

Result: in this tested Stage-3 corridor, both the extended beta-law gate and the sign-law gate pass. This strengthens the mechanistic statement for expressive models: entropy-controlled slope positivity is robust under cross-family extension, and partial-correlation direction is consistent inside the declared corridor.

\section{Stage-4 thermodynamic and theory checks}
We further stress the beta-law with a finite-size extrapolation and a lower-envelope (quantile regression) check:
\begin{itemize}
    \item finite-size cell fits of \(\beta_\mathrm{magic}(N)\) with weighted extrapolation to \(N\to\infty\),
    \item quantile regression (\(\tau=0.1,0.2,0.3\)) of validation NLL on magic and entropy controls as an empirical lower-bound proxy.
\end{itemize}

Outcome: both gates are positive in the tested Stage-3 corridor dataset: the all-cell thermodynamic extrapolation gate (\(\beta_{\infty,\mathrm{CI\,low}}>0\)) and the quantile lower-envelope gate are satisfied. This supports a stronger empirical law in the declared corridor, while remaining distinct from a theorem-level model-independent claim.

\section{Stage-5 external-family and regime stress}
We extend beyond Stage-4 by adding a fourth many-body family (ANNNI) with multiple additional regimes, then re-run corridor-gated beta-law and lower-envelope checks. The purpose is to test whether the Stage-4 mechanism survives adversarial cross-regime stress.

Outcome: family$\times$architecture cell-level gates remain positive (bootstrap lower bounds and permutation-FDR), and quantile lower-envelope positivity remains intact. However, strict all-regime minimax positivity fails due Schwinger \texttt{alt\_baseline} cells. This establishes a sharper boundary statement: the mechanism is robust across families in the declared corridor, but not globally regime-universal.

\section{Ablations}
We include seed, architecture, and regime ablations through the robustness suite and summarize outcomes in claim-gated reports. The primary endpoint is \texttt{magic $\rightarrow$ validation NLL $|$ entropy}; KL/TV are treated as secondary diagnostics. Negative controls (random-label and shuffled-time baselines) are included in the condition-level statistics to detect spurious correlation structure.

We additionally include positive-control synthetic state-family tests and mixed-effects regression analyses (reported in \texttt{report/statistical\_rigor.md}) to separate magic effects from entropy-only explanations.

\section{Limitations and next steps}
Current exact routines are restricted by Hilbert-space growth and Pauli enumeration cost. Practical next steps:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Use symmetry sectors and sparse block structure for larger $N$.
    \item Replace exact magic with Monte Carlo Pauli estimators at $N\gtrsim 12$.
    \item Extend NNQS architecture (Transformer/MADE) and include amplitude+phase learning from rotated measurement bases.
    \item Add Trotterized digital-circuit compilation for direct quantum hardware execution.
\end{enumerate}
